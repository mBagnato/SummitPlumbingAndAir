Heater Installation And Maintenance

Our goal is to simplify the process of selecting the right heater for your space by asking you targeted questions. This way, we can determine the most suitable solution for your needs and preferences. When providing a heater that fits your lifestyle, we take into account factors such as how you plan to use the heater, so that we can ensure that it meets your specific requirements.

  • The dimensions of the room, space, or building that requires heating.
  • How often the heating system will be used, for example, in a household with one person, a family of five, or a small business with a high volume of customers.
  • The purpose for which the heating system will be used (e.g. residential or light commercial).
  • Factors such as the initial budget and the ongoing operating costs should also be considered.
  • The energy efficiency rating - a higher rating means lower operating costs.
  • Whether the building is single or double storey and the level of access needed.
  • Duration of occupancy.

Gas Ducted Heating controls your heating through multiple outlets called ducts from one central system.

This system works by passing cold air over a heat exchanger which is heated by gas combustion within a unit in the roof space. This air is then forced out of the ducts to heat different areas of a building. Ductwork requires access to the ceiling or floor but can be hidden from view.

Ducted split systems with reverse cycle technology provide heating and cooling through a central unit called an air handler.

This unit is connected to a condenser unit installed outside the building, and the heated or cooled air is pushed through ducts in the ceiling to each room. The air temperature is generally consistent and the units are considered relatively quiet and discreet.

You don’t have to spend a fortune to create warmth in your home.

Depending on the unit, wall furnaces (heaters) create heat with gas or electricity in a compact, self-contained wall unit that’s blown out through its vents to heat the room. They’re a great option for small spaces, rental properties, and anyone looking for a cost saving, space saving heating option.

Heating Comparisons

Gas Ducted

Gas ducted heating is an excellent choice for homeowners who want a reliable and efficient heating system for their home. One of the main benefits of gas ducted heating is that it provides even heating throughout the house. The warm air is distributed evenly through ducts and vents, ensuring that every room is comfortable. This is especially important in larger homes, where other heating methods may struggle to provide consistent warmth throughout the entire space. Another advantage of gas ducted heating is that it is energy efficient. Natural gas is a relatively cheap fuel source compared to electricity, which means that homeowners can save money on their heating bills. Additionally, modern gas ducted heating systems have high energy efficiency ratings, which means that they use less gas to produce the same amount of heat compared to older systems.

Ducted Split

A ducted split system provides efficient and even heating and cooling throughout the home, using a central unit to distribute conditioned air via ductwork. With discreet installation, convenient control via a central thermostat, and long-term cost savings, it is a great choice for homeowners who want a reliable and energy-efficient heating and cooling system.

Wall Furnace

A wall furnace is a space-saving and efficient heating system that uses natural gas or propane to provide heat. With convenient control via a thermostat and long-term cost savings, it is a great choice for homeowners who want a reliable and cost-effective heating system.
