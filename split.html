Split System

Split-system air conditioners are commonly preferred by families and small businesses alike because they are cost-effective, easy to access, and require minimal effort to install and maintain. Consider the following factors when selecting a split-system air conditioner

  • The dimensions of the room, space, or building that requires heating.
  • How often the heating system will be used, for example, in a household with one person, a family of five, or a small business with a high volume of customers.
  • The purpose for which the heating system will be used (e.g. residential or light commercial).
  • Factors such as the initial budget and the ongoing operating costs should also be considered.
  • The energy efficiency rating - a higher rating means lower operating costs.
  • Whether the building is single or double storey and the level of access needed.
  • Duration of occupancy.

Cooling Comparisons

Ducted Split System

Ducted Split System is a type of air conditioning system that is designed to cool large residential or commercial spaces. The system consists of an outdoor unit that is connected to an indoor unit, which is usually installed in the ceiling or under the floor. The indoor unit is then connected to a network of ducts that run throughout the building, distributing cool air to different rooms or zones. This type of air conditioning system is ideal for those who want to cool multiple areas or rooms at the same time, as it allows for individual temperature control and can be zoned to cool only certain areas when needed. Ducted split systems are also known for their energy efficiency and quiet operation, making them a popular choice for many homeowners and businesses.

Wall Mount Split System

Wall-mounted split system air conditioners are a popular cooling option for many homes and businesses. They consist of two units: the indoor unit, which is mounted high up on a wall inside the building, and the outdoor unit, which can be installed in a number of positions on, alongside, or near an outside wall. These units are generally energy-efficient, quiet, and can be installed quickly by a professional. The wall-mounted design also allows for easy temperature control in a single room, making them a convenient choice for smaller spaces. They are a great choice for those looking for an affordable, easy-to-install cooling option.

Multihead Split System

Multihead split system air conditioners are an ideal option for those who want to cool multiple areas of their home or business. These systems consist of a single outdoor unit that connects to multiple indoor units, allowing for separate cooling zones. Multihead split systems are particularly useful for larger homes or offices that require different temperatures in different rooms. They also tend to be more energy-efficient than traditional air conditioning systems, as you can control the temperature in each room independently. Additionally, multihead split systems are easy to install and maintain, making them a popular choice for many homeowners and small business owners.

Cassette Split System

Cassette split system air conditioners are a popular option for large spaces such as offices, showrooms, galleries, and retail spaces. They are installed in the ceiling, making them a discreet and unobtrusive option that blends in with the space's architecture. These systems offer efficient and consistent cooling, with the ability to create different zones for cooling different areas. Cassette air conditioners are known for being quiet and unobtrusive, with low noise levels and minimal vibrations. They can also be controlled remotely with a variety of options, including mobile apps, voice commands, or wall-mounted controllers. Overall, cassette split system air conditioners are a great option for those who prioritize efficiency, functionality, and aesthetics in their cooling solutions.
