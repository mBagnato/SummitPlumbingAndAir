Split System

Split-system air conditioners are a popular choice for many families and small businesses due to their affordability, accessibility, and easy installation and maintenance.

Here are some things to think about when you’re looking for a split-system air conditioner:

  • Size of the room / space / building to be heated
  • Frequency of use (i.e. one person, a family of five, or a small business with hundreds of customers a day?)
  • Type of use (i.e. residential vs light commercial)
  • Budget and ongoing running costs
  • Energy rating - the higher the rating, the lower the running costs
  • Single or double storey and access required
  • Length of tenancy

Ducted split systems are the creme de la creme of residential cooling.

As a split system, you get an outdoor unit that connects to the indoor unit. But because it is ducted, you then get the option of running the ducts into various rooms without needing to install a unit in every one. Another advantage is that the cooling is more powerful than a ductless split system. Of course, this comes at a higher purchase and installation cost, so this needs to be factored into your decision.

Wall mounted split system air conditioners consist of two units..

The indoor unit is mounted high up on a wall inside the building and connects to the outdoor unit which can be installed in a number of positions on, alongside, or near an outside wall. They’re generally quiet, energy efficient, and can be installed quite quickly by a professional, making them a popular choice for many families, landlords, and small businesses.

Cooling Comparisons

Ducted Split System

  • Individual cooling zones
  • No need for a unit on every wall
  • More expensive to purchase and install

Wall Mount Split System

  • Energy efficient
  • Quiet
  • Fast installation
  • Units and installation are generally quite affordable
  • Not effective for large spaces
  • Units can take up a lot of wall space in a small room

Multihead Split System

  • Offers individual cooling zones
  • Because individual units can be turned on and off, it can be more energy efficient
  • More complex to repair and maintain
  • Better for smaller spaces than one large space
  • Audible

Cassette Split System

  • Great for large spaces
  • Visually appealing with unit hidden in ceiling
  • Quiet
  • More complex installation and maintenance
  • Unable to zone temperatures for individual spaces
